What did I learn from this project…

This project reinforced how powerful the as-is journey map is for surfacing operational blindspots. The pain points, particularly around application ownership and silent re-uploads, were friction that agents had normalised, invisible to stakeholders until mapped in detail.

The dual-track structure of mapping current versus potential experience in parallel helped align stakeholders quickly, giving them an immediate visual contrast between what exists and what's possible.

Working across agents, brokers, and BPU teams taught me that friction almost always lives between teams, not within them.

"Designing for complex workflows isn't about adding more. It's about removing the unnecessary so critical decisions become simple, reliable, and confident."
